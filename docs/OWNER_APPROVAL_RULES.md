\# Owner Approval Rules



\## Core Governance Rule

The Ecommerce AI Agent Platform may operate autonomously for normal operational tasks, but it must never spend more money, change budgets, scale campaigns, approve major financial commitments, or change strategy without explicit owner approval.



\## Fully Autonomous Actions

Agents may automatically:

\- Research products

\- Score products

\- Generate brand assets

\- Generate UGC scripts

\- Generate product image prompts

\- Generate ad copy and creative briefs

\- Build product pages

\- Build website/store content

\- Analyse competitors

\- Analyse campaign data

\- Recommend optimisations

\- Draft influencer outreach

\- Send low-risk follow-ups

\- Handle standard customer support

\- Generate reports

\- Update internal logs

\- Prepare workflows for approval



\## Owner Approval Required

Owner approval is mandatory before any agent can:

\- Increase ad spend

\- Change campaign budgets

\- Scale campaigns

\- Launch paid campaigns

\- Commit to paid influencer collaborations

\- Approve commission agreements

\- Approve product seeding at scale

\- Approve supplier orders above owner-defined limits

\- Approve refund batches above owner-defined limits

\- Sign or accept contracts

\- Make major business strategy changes

\- Override licence, package, or tenant restrictions



\## Approval Gateway Flow

1\. Agent identifies action.

2\. Agent prepares recommendation.

3\. System checks risk, cost, and authority level.

4\. If approval is required, action is paused.

5\. Owner receives approval request.

6\. Owner approves or rejects.

7\. System executes only if approved.

8\. Result is logged.



\## Default Safety Rule

If approval status is missing, unclear, expired, or failed, the action must be rejected by default.



\## Client Protection Rule

For white-label clients, the client must never see internal approval logic, prompts, API keys, backend configuration, or system reasoning.
