## Email 3 — Day 3 — Credits & How Jobs Work

**Subject:** Getting the most from your credits

**Preview text:** How credits work, what "human review" means, and how to read your job output.

---

**Body:**

Hi [FIRST_NAME],

A few things that'll make Vantro much easier to use — especially as you start running more jobs.

---

**How credits work**

Each agent job consumes credits based on its complexity. Simple research tasks use fewer credits; deep multi-source analyses use more. You'll always see the estimated credit cost before you submit.

Here's what happens during a job:
- When you submit, credits are **reserved** from your balance
- When the job completes, the reserved amount is **consumed**
- If the job fails for any reason, the credits are **fully refunded**

You're never charged for a job that didn't deliver.

---

**What "human review" means**

Some outputs — specifically anything that touches financial decisions, large-scale operational changes, or actions with significant downstream impact — are automatically flagged for human review before they're delivered.

This isn't a delay or a bug. It's a safety feature.

Vantro's agents are built to handle research, content, and analysis. They're explicitly blocked from taking financial actions or making commitments on your behalf. When an output gets close to that territory, a human reviews it first. You get a better output, and nothing happens that shouldn't.

You'll see these jobs with a "pending review" status — they clear quickly.

---

**How to read your job output**

Go to **Jobs** in the left nav → click any completed job → you'll see:

- Full output (copy, export, or share)
- Status and timestamps
- Credits used

**A few things that make jobs better:**

- **Be specific in your prompts.** "Analyze our top 10 SKUs for reorder risk based on 30-day sell-through rates" gets a better output than "check inventory."
- **Batch similar tasks.** If you're doing competitor research on five brands, run them back to back — don't spread them across weeks.
- **Check history before re-running.** Your job history is searchable. Someone on your team may have already run the same thing.
