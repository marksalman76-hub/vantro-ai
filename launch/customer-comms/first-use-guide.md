# Getting Started with Vantro

---

## 1. What Is Vantro?

Vantro gives your e-commerce team a workforce of 27 AI agents that handle the research, content, analytics, operations, and growth work that normally takes days — in minutes. Describe a task in plain language, submit it, and receive structured, actionable output you can act on immediately.

Vantro is built for e-commerce professionals: heads of e-commerce, marketing managers, and ops leads at DTC brands, retailers, and marketplace sellers. Whether you need a competitor deep-dive, a batch of product descriptions, a demand forecast, or a campaign plan, there is an agent for it.

You do not need to write prompts, configure models, or manage any infrastructure. You describe what you need. Vantro handles the rest.

---

## 2. Your First 5 Minutes

Follow these steps to run your first agent job from sign-up to output.

1. **Sign up and create your account**
   Go to [vantro.ai](https://vantro.ai), enter your email, and set a password. Confirm your email when prompted.

2. **Create a workspace**
   A workspace is your team's shared environment. Name it after your brand, business unit, or project (e.g., "Acme DTC" or "EMEA Team"). You can create additional workspaces later.

3. **Browse the agent catalogue**
   From your dashboard, open the **Agent Catalogue**. Agents are organized into five categories: Research, Content, Analytics, Operations, and Growth. Scan the categories to get a feel for what is available.

4. **Pick an agent and describe your task**
   Click any agent to open it. You will see a description of what the agent does, the credit cost estimate, and a task input field. Write your task in plain language — the more specific, the better.

5. **Submit the job**
   Click **Run Agent**. Your job will appear in the **Jobs** section with a status of *Pending*. Most jobs complete within 1–3 minutes.

6. **Read your output**
   When the job is complete, click it in the Jobs list to open the full output. Results are structured reports with sections, tables, and recommendations ready to use.

> **Pro tip: be specific.**
> Instead of *"research my competitors"*, try *"analyze the pricing strategy and top product categories of [competitor.com] compared to our store, focusing on their bestsellers under $50."*
> Specific tasks produce specific, usable output.

## 3. Understanding Credits

**What credits are**
Credits are the currency for running agent jobs on Vantro. Every job has a credit cost based on the complexity and depth of the task.

**How they work**

- Before you submit a job, you will see the estimated credit cost.
- Credits are reserved when the job starts.
- Credits are consumed when the job completes successfully.
- If a job fails for any reason, credits are automatically refunded to your account — no action needed on your part.

**How to get more credits**

- **Top up anytime:** Go to **Billing** and purchase a credit pack. Credits are added to your account immediately.
- **Monthly allowance:** Subscribing to a plan gives you a larger credit allowance that refreshes each billing cycle.

**Checking your balance**
Your current credit balance is always visible in the top navigation bar.

> **Note:** Credits do not expire on monthly plans. Unused credits roll over as long as your subscription is active.

## 4. The Agent Catalogue

Vantro's 27 agents are organized into five categories. Here is what each category covers and some examples of the agents inside.

---

### Research Agents

**What they do:** Deep dives into markets, competitors, suppliers, and emerging trends. Use these agents when you need intelligence before making a decision — launching a product, entering a market, vetting a supplier, or understanding what a competitor is doing.

**Example agents:**
- **Competitor Intelligence Agent** — Analyzes a competitor's product range, pricing, positioning, and customer sentiment.
- **Market Trend Analyzer** — Identifies rising trends in your category using search signals, social data, and market indicators.
- **Supplier Discovery Agent** — Surfaces and evaluates potential suppliers based on your product type, region, and requirements.

---

### Content Agents

**What they do:** Generate, optimize, and repurpose content for product pages, paid ads, and email campaigns. Use these agents to scale content production without sacrificing quality or brand voice.

**Example agents:**
- **Product Description Writer** — Creates SEO-optimized product descriptions in your brand's tone, from a brief or a URL.
- **Ad Copy Generator** — Produces headline and body copy variants for Meta, Google, and TikTok ads, ready to test.
- **Email Campaign Builder** — Drafts full email sequences — promotional, post-purchase, or re-engagement — from a brief.

---

### Analytics Agents

**What they do:** Analyze your data, surface actionable insights, and produce structured reports. Use these agents to make sense of performance data without spending hours in spreadsheets.

**Example agents:**
- **Sales Performance Analyst** — Breaks down revenue by product, channel, period, and customer segment, then flags what is driving or dragging performance.
- **Customer Cohort Analyzer** — Compares cohort retention, LTV, and purchase frequency to identify your most and least valuable customer segments.
- **Ad Spend Attribution Advisor** — Reviews cross-channel ad spend and surfaces efficiency gaps, wasted budget, and reallocation recommendations.

---

### Operations Agents

**What they do:** Streamline fulfillment, inventory planning, and supplier workflows. Use these agents to reduce stock-outs, manage returns, and prepare for negotiations.

**Example agents:**
- **Inventory Demand Forecaster** — Projects demand by SKU and season based on your sales history and external signals.
- **Return Analysis Agent** — Analyzes return rate patterns by product, customer segment, and reason code, and recommends fixes.
- **Supplier Negotiation Briefer** — Prepares a structured briefing document for supplier negotiations, including benchmarks, leverage points, and suggested terms.

---

### Growth Agents

**What they do:** Find growth opportunities, optimize conversion, and plan campaigns. Use these agents to prioritize where to focus and build out plans quickly.

**Example agents:**
- **Growth Opportunity Scout** — Scans your category for untapped segments, whitespace products, and expansion opportunities.
- **Conversion Rate Optimizer** — Reviews your funnel and product pages, then produces a prioritized list of CRO recommendations.
- **Seasonal Campaign Planner** — Builds a full campaign calendar and brief for a seasonal event, including angles, channels, and timeline.

## 5. Reading Agent Output

**Where to find results**
Go to **Dashboard → Jobs** and click any completed job to open the full output.

**What a completed job looks like**
Outputs are structured reports — not raw text. Depending on the agent, you will typically see organized sections, comparison tables, prioritized recommendations, and clear next steps. You can copy, export, or share the output directly.

**Job statuses explained**

| Status | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Pending** | Your job is queued and will start shortly. |
| **Running** | The agent is actively working on your task. |
| **Completed** | Output is ready. Click the job to view results. |
| **Failed** | Something went wrong. Credits are automatically refunded. |
| **Under Review** | A team member is reviewing the output before it is released to you. You will be notified when it is ready. |

**A note on "Under Review"**
For certain high-impact outputs — particularly those involving significant financial decisions or large-scale recommendations — Vantro applies a human review step before the output is released. This is a safety measure, not an error. It typically resolves within a few hours. You do not need to take any action; you will receive a notification when your results are ready.

## 6. Inviting Your Team

Vantro workspaces are designed for teams. Everyone in a workspace shares the credit pool and can view all job history.

**To invite team members:**
1. Go to **Settings → Team**
2. Click **Invite Members**
3. Enter their email addresses and assign roles

**Roles:**
- **Member** — Can run agents, view jobs, and read outputs.
- **Admin** — Full member access plus the ability to manage integrations, billing, and approve high-stakes jobs before they run.

> **Tip:** Create one workspace per brand or department to keep job history, credits, and integrations cleanly separated.

---

## 7. Connecting Your Tools

Vantro integrates with your existing e-commerce stack. Connected tools allow agents to pull real-time data — your actual sales numbers, ad performance, inventory levels — for more accurate and personalized outputs.

**To connect an integration:**
1. Go to **Settings → Integrations**
2. Select the platform you want to connect
3. Follow the authorization steps

Vantro supports connections to store platforms, analytics tools, and marketing platforms. The full list is available in the Integrations section of your settings.

**Using Vantro without integrations**
Integrations are optional. Without them, agents work using publicly available data and the context you provide in your task description. Connecting your tools improves output quality — especially for analytics and operations agents — but you can get meaningful results right away.
